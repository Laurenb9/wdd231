About Me

Hi! My name is Laura Rendón, and I’m from Colombia. I’m currently studying Web Development at BYU, where I’m learning how to build websites and understand the foundations of dynamic, modern applications. Technology has always caught my attention, not only because of its impact on the world but also because it allows me to create, solve problems, and express ideas in unique ways. Outside of school, one of my greatest passions is boxing. Training has taught me discipline, focus, and resilience—qualities that I also apply to my academic and professional life. I also love art and creative projects, especially when I can connect them with technology. Mixing creativity with logic makes me feel inspired, and I try to bring that mindset into everything I do. My long-term goal is to grow as a software developer and eventually work in the aerospace or defense technology fields. I find it exciting to think about contributing to projects that push human limits and have a real impact on the future. A fun fact about me is that I’m always looking for balance between structure and creativity. Whether I’m designing a web page, training in the gym, or sketching ideas, I feel happiest when I can combine both worlds. This course is a small step in that bigger journey, and I’m excited to see how much I can grow along the way.
